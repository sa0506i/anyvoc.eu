Anyvoc stands on the shoulders of excellent open-source libraries and publicly released language data. This page lists the most relevant third-party sources and their licenses, with thanks to their authors and maintainers.

Language and data sources

Word frequency tables

Source: Leipzig Corpora Collection, Leipzig University.

Goldhahn, D., Eckart, T., & Quasthoff, U. (2012). Building Large Monolingual Dictionaries at the Leipzig Corpora Collection: From 100 to 200 Languages. In Proceedings of the 8th International Conference on Language Resources and Evaluation (LREC 2012).
  • License: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) — creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/
  • URL: wortschatz.uni-leipzig.de
  • Used for: the on-device CEFR-level model across all 12 supported languages.

Age-of-Acquisition norms (English)

Kuperman, V., Stadthagen-Gonzalez, H., & Brysbaert, M. (2012). Age-of-acquisition ratings for 30,000 English words. Behavior Research Methods, 44(4), 978–990.

Used as the second feature of the English CEFR classifier.

Age-of-Acquisition estimates (11 other languages)

Generated offline with a language model on the Kuperman scale (2–18) because no comparably large human-norm data set has been published for these languages. The scale follows the methodology of Kuperman et al. 2012, with no claim of identical validity.
